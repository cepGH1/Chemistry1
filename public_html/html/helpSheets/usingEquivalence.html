Relative amounts reacting

Examples

1. How many moles of sulphur would be needed to completely react 1 mole of magnesium to make magnesium sulphide

  • Word Equation
    magnesium + sulphur → magnesium sulphide
  • Formula Equation
    Mg + S --> MgS
  • One molecule of magnesium reacts with 1 molecule of sulphur
  • One mole of magnesium reacts with one mole of sulphur

2. How many moles of sodium hydroxide would be needed to completely neutralise 1 mole of sulphuric acid

  • Word Equation
    sulphuric acid + sodium hydroxide --> sodium sulphate + water
  • Formula Equation
    H2SO4 + 2NaOH → Na2SO4 + 2H2O
  • One molecule of sulphuric acid reacts with 2 molecules of sodium hydroxide
  • One mole of sulphuric acid reacts with two moles of sodium hydroxide
