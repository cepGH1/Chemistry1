How to work out the concentration of a solution

The formula to do this is

  1. Calculate the Relative Formula Mass of the solute

  2. Calculate the number of moles of the solute

  3. Convert the volume of solvent to dm3

  4. Divide moles of solute/volume of solvent

Examples

1. Calculate the concentration of the solution that would result from dissolving 100g of sodium chloride in 2dm3 water.

  1. Relative Formula Mass of solute, sodium chloride NaCl
    22.99 + 35.45 = 58.44
  2. Number of moles of solute
    mass/relative mass = 100/58.44 = 1.711 mol
  3. Volume of solvent(water) in dm3
    2dm3
  4. Concentration
    moles of solute/volume of solvent = 1.711/2 = 0.856 mol/dm3

2. What would be the concentration, of the brine produced, if 25g of sodium chloride were to be dissolved in 200cm3 water?

  1. Relative Formula Mass of solute, sodium chloride NaCl
    22.99 + 35.45 = 58.44
  2. Number of moles of solute
    mass/relative mass = 25/58.44 = 0.428 mol
  3. Volume of solvent(water) in dm3
    200/1000 = 0.2dm3
  4. Concentration
    moles of solute/volume of solvent in dm3 = 0.428/0.2 = 2.14 mol/dm3
