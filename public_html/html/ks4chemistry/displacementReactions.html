Displacement Reactions

A more reactive element will displace a less reactive element from its salt.

Metals

A more reactive metal with displace a less reactive metal from it's salts.

Example

Magnesium + Zinc Nitrate → Magnesium Nitrate + Zinc

Mg(s) + Zn(NO3)2(aq) → Mg(NO3)2(aq) + Zn(s)

Uses of displacement reactions

Thermite reaction
Aluminium displaces Iron from Iron Oxide. The reaction is so exothermic that the resulting Iron is molten. The reaction is used as a source of liquid Iron to chemically weld railway track together
2Al(s) + Fe2O3(s) → Al2O3(s) + 2Fe(l)

Magnesium sacrificial protection against corrosion
Blocks of Magnesium metal are sometimes attached to Iron hulls of ships. The Magnesium displaces the Iron out of any rust that forms.
3Mg → 3Mg2+ + 6e-
2Fe3+ + 6e- → 2Fe
Writing this as half equations is important
The reaction is like a displacement reaction, but the electrons taking part in the reaction travel all over the hull in small currents. The block doesn't need to be in direct contact with rust molecule from which the iron is displaced.

Halogens

A more reactive halogen will displace a less reactive halogen from it's halide solution.

Example

Bubbling pale green chlorine gas through a clear solution of sodium bromide results in a brown solution. The Chlorine takes the electron from the bromide ions and becomes chloride. The bromine resumes its elemental brown colour.

Cl2(g) + 2NaBr(aq) --> Br2(l) + 2NaCl(aq)
