Combustion Reactions

Combustion = burning in air or oxygen

A compound or element reacts with oxygen

Oxides are produced

Combustion of alkanes

Hydrocarbons burn to give the oxides of the elements carbon and hydrogen. Depending on the amount of oxygen available, water (hydrogen oxide) is produced along with either carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide or carbon

Lots of oxygen available

When there is excess(lots of) oxygen. Carbon dioxide and water are formed.

Example
Burning methane.
methane + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water
CH4(g) + 2O2(g) → CO2(g) + 2H2O(g)

Less oxygen available

When there is a limited supply of oxygen, carbon monoxide is produced instead of carbon dioxide. Carbon monoxide is poisonous.

Example
methane + oxygen → carbon monoxide + water
2CH4(g) + 3O2(g) → 2CO(g) + 4H2O(g)

Very little oxygen available

Carbon does not get to react with the oxygen. The oxygen is used up reacting with hydrogen. Carbon and water are the products of this reaction

Example
methane + oxygen → carbon + water
CH4(g) + O2(g) → C(s) + 2H2O(g)

Reaction of elements with oxygen

Oxides are produced

carbon + oxygen → carbon dioxide

C(s) + O2(g) → CO2(g)
