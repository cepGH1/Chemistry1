Crude Oil

Crude oil is a mixture of organic compounds, mostly hydrocarbons. Mined from the ground from oil wells or fracking.

Creation

Crude oil was created, millions of years ago, by incomplete rotting of small marine animals and plants. Their bodies got covered by layers of sand and mud. The bodies broke down in the absence oxygen. With time, heat and pressure the substance turned into oil and gas.

Uses

Hydrocarbons react with oxygen. They are fuels. They burn. These reactions release lots of energy. We use this energy for many things.
The hydrocarbons in crude oil are also used to make other useful chemicals. They are feedstock for the petrochemical industry

Limited Resource

Oil takes millions of years to be created, but is used very quickly. Oil is a limited resource and we can and will run out of it during the 21st century.

Oil Refining

The crude oil mixture is separated out by fractional distillation (oil refining).
The products that come from crude oil include
  • fuel oil
  • kerosene (air craft fuel)
  • diesel
  • petrol
  • lighter fluid
  • chemical feedstocks
