Empirical Formula

The simplest form of the formula of a compound.

The formulae of salts are usually given in their empirical form.
This tells you how many metal ions there are compared to non-metal ions in the compound.
eg. sodium chloride NaCl
One Chloride ion for every Sodium ion.
Magnesium Chloride MgCl2
Two Chloride ions for every one Magnesium ion

Covalently bonded molecules(such as organic molecules made with carbon and hydrogen) are not normally described with their empirical formula.
Their molecular formula is commonly used.
Working out the empirical formula of a largish molecule is a common exam question
