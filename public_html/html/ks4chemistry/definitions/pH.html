pH

pH

A measure of how acidic or alkaline a solution is.
This measures the hydrogen ion concentration in a solution.
The pH is calculated using this formula

pH = -log10[H+]

pH is - the log to the base 10 of the hydrogen ion (proton) concentration

Measurement

pH can be measured using a pH meter or by using universal indicator solution. This changes colour as pH changes.
red = acid pH1
green = neutral pH7
purple = alkaline pH14

Indicators

An indicator is any compound that changes colour at a particular pH.
They can be very sensitive and precise.

Examples of Indicators

Litmus

Red in acids and blue in alkalis

Methyl Orange

Red in acid and yellow in alkali

Phenolphthalein

Clear in acid and puce(purple pink) in alkali
Universal Indicator is made from a mixture of other specific types of indicator.
