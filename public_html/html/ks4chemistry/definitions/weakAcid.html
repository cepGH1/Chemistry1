Weak Acid

An acid that does not completely dissociate in aqueous solution.

A weak acid produces less H + ions in solution than the same amount of a strong acid

A weak acid's pH is not so low as a strong acid's pH

Because these acids are less extreme in pH they are less likely to cause acid burns

How does this happen?
If the acid has the formula HA
When it is dissolved or in liquid form, the following would happen;

HA ⇌ H + + A -

A reversible reaction is taking place. Some of the HA splits apart to form ions (releasing hydrogen ions). Some remains as HA.

Real Example
Ethanoic Acid ( CH3COOH )

CH3COOH ⇌ CH3COO - + H +
