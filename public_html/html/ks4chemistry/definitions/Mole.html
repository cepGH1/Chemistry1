Mole

The mole is a measurement of the amount of an element or compound.

One mole is the amount of a substance that has Avagadro's number of items in it.

One mole of Sodium has Avagadro's number of Sodium atoms in it.
(That is 6.022 x 1023 Sodium atoms)

One mole of Argon has Avagadro's number of Argon atoms in it (6.022 x 1023 Argon atoms)

One mole of Methane has Avagadro's number of Methane molecules in it (6.022 x 1023 Methane molecules).

The mass of 1 mole of a substance is it's Relative Formula Mass in grams.

1 mole of Sodium weighs 22.990g
(Sodium's Relative Atomic Mass is 22.990)
6.022 x 1023 atoms of Sodium weigh 22.990 grams.

1 mole of Methane weighs 16.043g
(Formula : CH4
Carbon Relative Atomic Mass : 12.011
Hydrogen Relative Atomic Mass : 1.008
Relative Formula Mass (1 x 12.011) + (4 x 1.008) = 16.043)
6.022 x 1023 molecules of Methane weigh 16.043g

The mole is an SI unit and it's symbol is mol
