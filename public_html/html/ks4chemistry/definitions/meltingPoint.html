Melting Point

The temperature at which a solid substance changes phase to become a liquid

Each pure substance has it's own melting point. This is one way to help identify a substance

Melting uses energy (takes in heat energy from the surroundings).

If a substance is heated the temperature rises until the melting point is reached and then stays the same while melting happens. After melting the temperature rises again with heating.

Mixtures and impure substances don't have a single sharp melting point. The melt over a range of temperature

Needing energy to melt explains why ice cools a drink.
The ice absorbs heat energy from your drink in order to melt
