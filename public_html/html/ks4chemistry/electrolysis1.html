Electrolysis

The process of passing electricity through a liquid that contains ions.
This process causes chemical reactions to happen at the electrodes.

Glossary

  • electrode - the point in the electric circuit that is in contact with the liquid
  • anode - positive electrode (attractive to electrons and negative ions)
  • cathode - negative electrode (attractive to positively charged ions)
  • electrolyte - substance dissolved or melted forming the conducting liquid in a circuit doing electrolysis
  • reduction - addition of electrons - usually the cathode process
  • oxidation - loss of electrons - usually the anode process
