Transition Metals

Metals located in the transition are not so reactive as the group 1 and 2 metals. Mostly they are dense metals. Most are hard, but there are exceptions, Mercury and Gold. Many of these elements have been in use as metals since ancient times.
Many of the transition metals have coloured ions.They make coloured compounds.
Often they can have more than one type of ion (multiple oxidation states).
Some transition metals are useful as catalysts. For example, the iron catalyst used in the Haber process
