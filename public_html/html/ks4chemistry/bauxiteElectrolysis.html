Extraction of Reactive Metals by Electrolysis

Reactive metals cannot be displaced from their oxides using Carbon or Carbon Monoxide because they are above Carbon in the reactivity series

The metals can be extracted by passing electric current through their salts after first melting the salts. Remember salts are made up of charged ions. They have a infinite lattice structure in the solid state and the ions cannot move Once melted, though, the ions can move about. The ions are attracted to places that have opposite charge to their own. Here, at the electrodes, they either gain or loose electrons and are turned back into elements.
