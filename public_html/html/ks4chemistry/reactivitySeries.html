Reactivity Series (of metals)

A list of metals(sometimes with carbon and hydrogen as well), placed in order of how reactive they are. Those at the top are most likely to take part in chemical reactions. Those at the bottom are least likely to undergo a chemical reaction.
The reactive metals at the top of this series are usually only seen in compounds.
Displacement Reactions
A more reactive metal will displace a less reactive metal from it's salt in solution
Example
Magnesium + Copper Sulphate → Magnesium Sulphate + Copper
Mg(s) + CuSO4(aq) → MgSO4(aq) + Cu(s)

When a silver piece of Magnesium metal is dipped into a blue solution of Copper Sulphate, the solution gradually becomes colourless and a brown deposit of Copper appears.
