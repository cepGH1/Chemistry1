Concentration

Working out molar concentrations

  1. Convert all volumes to decimeters cubed dm3

    1dm3 = 1000cm3

  2. Work out the number of moles of substance

    Moles = mass / relative molecular mass

  3. Calculate concentration

    Divide the number of moles by the volume
    concentration = moles/volume

Examples

Example 1
Calculate the concentration in moles per dm3 of a salt water solution where 58.5g of sodium chloride are dissolved in 500cm3 water

  1. Volume in dm3

    Convert 500cm3 to dm3
    Divide by 1000
    Volume = 500/1000 = 0.5dm3

  2. Number of Moles

    Molecular mass of sodium chloride
    Formula is NaCl
    Relative atomic mass chlorine is 35.5
    Relative atomic mass of sodium is 23.0
    Relative molecular mass NaCl = 35.5 + 23.0 = 58.5
    Moles = mass/relative molecular mass
    Moles = 58.5/58.5 = 1mol

  3. Concentration

    concentration = moles/volume
    concentration = 1/0.5 = 2.0mol/dm3
    = 2M
