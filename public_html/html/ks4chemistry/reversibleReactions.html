⇌ Reversible Reactions ⇌

Some special reactions can happen in both directions
The products of the reaction can react together to remake the reactants
Two reactions happen at the same time
Reactants → Products
and
Products → Reactants
or
Reactants ← Products
There is a special sign instead of an arrow that we use to show that a reaction is happening in both forwards and reverse direction at the same time

⇌

Equilibrium

The reactions reach a point where the forward and backward reaction are happening at the same rate. This is called equilibrium. At equilibrium there will be a constant amount of reactants and a constant amount of products in the reaction mixture.
When conditions change the rates of the reactions may change and the amounts of reactants and products in the reactions mixture may change. the equlibrium is then said to have changed position. The way the equilibrium position changes is predicted using Le Chatelier's Principle

Example

2N2(g) + 3H2(g) ⇌ 2NH3(g)

Nitrogen and Hydrogen are reacting to form Ammonia. At the same time, Ammonia is decomposing to form Hydrogen and Nitrogen.

Haber Process
The Haber process makes Ammonia from Nitrogen and Hydrogen.
Contact Process
The Contact process makes Sulphuric Acid from Sulphur, Oxygen and Sulphuric Acid/Water
