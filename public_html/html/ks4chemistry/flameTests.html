Flame Tests

When some ions are put into a flame they change the flame's colour
Not all elements produce a visible light flame colour
When they do you can tell which element it is
Why does a specific colour get produced?
In the flame the ion takes in energy. The energy moves electrons to higher energy levels in the ion. The levels each have specific energy (they are quantised).
When electrons fall back to their original level they give out the excess energy that they took in to jump up. This happens to be energy, in photon form, and it can have the wavelength of visible light. We see this energy as the flame colour.
Spectrophotometry
A fancy term for measuring the colour of an element or compound.
Either the colour of light it absorbs or the colour of light it does not absorb or the colour of light it emits .
