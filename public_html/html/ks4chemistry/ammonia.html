Ammonia NH3

A noxious gas.
Ammonia is a base and accepts a hydrogen during chemical reactions to become the Ammonium ion NH4+
Example
Ammonia + Hydrogen Chloride → Ammonium Chloride
NH4OH(g) + HCl(g) → NH4Cl(s)

Ammonia is very soluble and is usually met as a solution in water. The solution is pH9 and this too is a base. Ammonia solution chemical formula NH4OH (Ammonium Hydroxide) reacts with acids to form ammonium salt and water.
Example
Sulphuric Acid Acid + Ammonia → Ammonium Sulphate + Water
H2SO4(aq) + 2NH4OH(aq) → (NH4)2SO4 + 2H2O
