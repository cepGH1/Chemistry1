Ammonia NH3

Ammonia is a noxious gas.
Ammonia is a base and accepts a hydrogen during chemical reactions to become the Ammonium ion NH4+
Example
Ammonia + Hydrogen Chloride → Ammonium Chloride
NH3(g) + HCl(g) → NH4Cl(s)

Ammonium Hydroxide

Ammonia is very soluble in water. The solution of ammonia in water is ammonium hydroxide, but it is usually just called ammonia. The solution has a pH of 9 and, this too, is a base. Ammonia solution, NH4OH (Ammonium Hydroxide), reacts with acids to form ammonium salts and water. This is an example of a neutralisation reaction.
Example
Sulphuric Acid + Ammonia → Ammonium Sulphate + Water
H2SO4(aq) + 2NH4OH(aq) → (NH4)2SO4 + 2H2O
