Carbon Monoxide CO

A poisonous gas. Colourless and odourless.

Produced when hydrocarbons, wood, charcoal or coal is burned in low oxygen conditions

Example

2C2H6 + 5O2 --> 4CO + 6H2O

Toxicity

Carbon monoxide binds to the haem group of haemoglobin, in blood, where oxygen normally would. Unlike oxygen, carbon monoxide sticks permanently to haem. The haem becomes completely used up and oxygen cannot reach the body's cells and organs. The victim dies from lack of oxygen.
