Calculating Empirical Formula

How to work out Empirical Formula from Displayed Formula

Example 1 Ethane

  1. Count the number of element atoms
    2 carbons and 6 hydrogens
  2. List them in the order C, H, N, O
  3. Put the numbers, after the element symbol and lower
    C2H6
    This is the molecular formula
  4. If the number of hydrogens can be divided by the number of carbons (exactly)
    Divide through by the number of carbons.
    C2/2H6/2 = CH3
  5. CH3 is the simplest formula that tells you how many carbons there are compared to hydrogens in ethane. This is the Empirical Formula

Example 2 Ethanoic Acid

  1. Count the number of element atoms
    2 carbons, 4 hydrogens, and 2 oxygens
  2. List them in the order C, H, N, O
  3. Put the numbers, after the element symbol and lower
    C2H4O2
    This is the molecular formula of ethanoic acid
  4. If the number of hydrogens can be divided by the number of carbons (exactly)
    Divide through by the number of carbons.
    C2/2H4/2O2/2 = CH2O
  5. CH2O is the simplest formula that tells you how many carbons there are compared to hydrogens and oxygens, in ethanoic acid. This is the Empirical Formula of ethanoic acid

Example 3 Ethane-1,2-diol

  1. Count the number of element atoms
    2 carbons, 6 hydrogens, and 2 oxygens
  2. List them in the order C, H, N, O
  3. Put the numbers, after the element symbol and lower
    C2H6O2
    This is the molecular formula of ethane-1,2-diol
  4. If the number of hydrogens and oxygens can be divided by the number of carbons (exactly)
    Divide through by the number of carbons.
    C2/2H6/2O2/2 = CH3O
  5. CH3O is the simplest formula that tells you how many carbons there are compared to hydrogens and oxygens, in ethane-1,2-diol. This is the Empirical Formula of ethane-1,2-diol.

Example 4 Butane

The simplification of a molecular formula to an empirical formula
Sometimes you can't divide through to reduce any element to 1
  1. Count the number of element atoms
    4 carbons, 10 hydrogens
  2. List them in the order C, H, N, O
  3. Put the numbers, after the element symbol and lower
    C4H10
    This is the molecular formula of butane
  4. If the number of hydrogens and oxygens can be divided by any number (exactly)
    Divide through by the largest number possible.
    C4/2H10/2 = C2H5
  5. C2H5 is the simplest formula that tells you how many carbons there are compared to hydrogens in butane. This is the Empirical Formula of butane.
