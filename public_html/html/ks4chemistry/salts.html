Salts

This is the name given to compounds made up of positive and negative ions (cations and anions)
Metal cations and non-metal anions
In solids the ions are held together by electrostatic attraction. Thats because positive things are attracted to negative things.

Example - sodium chloride

NaCl

Solubility

If a solvent has some charge then ions can stick to that instead and they leave the solid to hang around in the solvent. They dissolve. Water has a slight charge (a dipole) and this is enough for a lot of ionic compounds to be pulled apart. Many ionic compounds dissolve in water.
All nitrates are soluble
