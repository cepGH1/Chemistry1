Answers to Sodium Questions

  1. The mineral oil prevents the Sodium coming into contact with air. Sodium reacts instantaneously with oxygen and water vapour in air.
    • wear safety spectacles
    • wear a lab coat
    • wear gloves
    • work in a fume cupboard if possible, where any fire can be dealt with without spreading it across the laboratory
    • Use a screen between the experiment and other people in the lab
  3. Sodium floats on the water and reacts vigorously with the water. The blob of sodium can be seen to melt and become a round drop of liquid sodium. The sodium may burn with a yellow flame if lit using a lighted taper.
    The sodium reacts with the water to produce sodium hydroxide solution and hydrogen gas.
    Sodium + Water --> Sodium Hydroxide + Hydrogen
    2Na(s) + 2H2O(l) --> 2NaOH(aq) + H2(g)
  4. Sodium Reacting with Chlorine
    Equation
    Sodium + Chlorine --> Sodium Chloride
    2Na(s) + Cl2(g) --> 2NaCl(s)
    Relative Masses
    2x23 + 35.5x2 --> 2(23 + 35.5)
    46 + 71 --> 117
    46g + 71g ---> 117g
    46g of Sodium would produce 117g of sodium chloride
  5. yellow/orange flame
  6. Two moles of sodium produces 1 mole of hydrogen
    One mole of sodium would produce 1/2 mole of hydrogen
    One mole of gas at standard temperature and pressure has volume 22.4dm3
    11.2dm3 Hydrogen would be produced
