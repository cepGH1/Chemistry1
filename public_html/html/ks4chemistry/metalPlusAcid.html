Reactions of metals with acids

The Reactivity Series of Metals

How a metal reacts with an acid depends on how reactive the metal is.

Metals are shown in order of their reactivity by the reactivity series

Metals above Hydrogen

Metals that are above hydrogen in the reactivity series react with acids to produce a salt and hydrogen gas. They displace the hydrogen from the acid and take it's anion to become a salt.

magnesium + hydrochloric acid --> magnesium chloride + hydrogen

Mg(s) + 2HCl(aq) --> MgCl2(aq) + H2(g)

lithium + nitric acid --> lithium nitrate + hydrogen

2Li(s) + 2HNO3(aq) --> 2LiNO3(aq) + H2(g)

Metals below Hydrogen

Metals that are below hydrogen in the reactivity series do not react with dilute acids at all, because they cannot displace the hydrogen from the acid

Some of these metals dissolve in concentrated sulfuric or nitric acid.
