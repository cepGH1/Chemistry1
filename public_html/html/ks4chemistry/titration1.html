Titration

Method used to work out the concentration of a solution by reacting it with a solution of known concentration. Often acids are reacted with alkalis in this method.

Working out a titration

  1. Write a word equation

  2. Write a balanced formula equation

  3. Work out the molar equivalences

    How many moles of the unknown reacts with 1 mole of the standard

  4. Work out how many moles of the standard substance has been used

  5. Work out how many moles of the unknown have been reacted

  6. Work out the concentration of the unknown

Titration Calculation Examples

Example 1
35cm3 of 1M hydrochloric acid was used to completely neutralise a 250cm3 sample of sodium hydroxide solution. What was the concentration of the sodium hydroxide solution?

  1. Word Equation

    Hydrochloric acid + sodium hydroxide --> sodium chloride + water

  2. Formula Equation

    HCl(aq) + NaOH(aq) --> NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)

  3. molar equivalence

    1:1
    1 mole of hydrochloric acid neutralises 1 mole of sodium hydroxide

  4. How many moles have been used in this reaction

    We know the concentration and the volume of the hydrochloric acid
    Concentration = 1M (1 mole per decimeter cubed, dm3)
    Volume = 35cm3 = 35/1000dm3 = 0.035dm3
    All volumes need to be converted and used in dm3
    Number of moles of hydrochloric acid (conc x volume = moles)
    = 1 x 0.035 = 0.035 moles of HCl

  5. How many moles of the unknown would be neutralised

    The equivalence HCl:NaOH is 1:1, so
    0.035 moles of HCl would neutralise 0.035 moles of NaOH

  6. Concentration of the unknown solution

    concentration = moles/volume
    moles = 0.035 moles
    volume = 250cm3 = 250/1000 dm3 = 0.25dm3
    concentration = 0.035/0.25 = 0.14M

Titration Questions

  2. Work out the concentration of a solution of sodium hydroxide if 25cm3 of this solution were completely and exactly neutralised by 25cm3 of a 1M sulfuric acid solution.

  3. Work out the concentration of a solution of sodium hydroxide if 25cm3 of this solution were completely and exactly neutralised by 25cm3 of a 1M nitric acid solution.
