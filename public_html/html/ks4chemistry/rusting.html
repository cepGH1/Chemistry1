Rusting

The reaction of Iron, with Oxygen and water, to form iron oxides is called rusting
Rusting weakens objects made from iron, because iron oxide (rust) is powdery and very weak. Iron structures and objects need to be protected from rusting
Rusting can be prevented in several ways
  1. Don't let oxygen contact the iron. Coat the iron with oil so the oxygen cant touch the iron
  2. Use a more reactive metal to react with the oxygen before it gets to the iron. Ingots of magnesium are attached to iron hulls of ships for this reason
  3. Don't allow water to have contact with the iron
