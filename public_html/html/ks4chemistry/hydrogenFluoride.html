Hydrogen Fluoride

Hydrogen Fluoride is a gas at room temperature, just. It's boiling point is 19.5 degrees centigrade. Just like water it has a high boiling point because there are hydrogen bonds between the molecules.

HF is an acid. HF reacts with glass. HF is risky to work with as it causes burns and reacts with bones, displacing the calcium from them and leaving them jelly like.

HF is used in industries that require glass and silicon to be chemically modified.

HF is a covalently bonded diatomic molecule.
