Equilibrium Answers

  1. A reversible reaction is one in which the products can react together to recreate the reactants. The two reactions, forwards to make products, and backwards to make reactants happen at the same time in the reaction mixture. When the forward and backward rate are the same the reaction is at equilibrium.
  2. When the forward and backward rates of reaction of a reversible reaction are the same, there are constant amounts of reactants and products in the reaction mixture. The reaction is at equilibrium.
