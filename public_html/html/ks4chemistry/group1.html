Alkali Metals,
Group 1

Reactions

Alkali metals react to form white or colourless, soluble, salts.
They react with many nonmetal elements and with water and with all acids.
They form +1 ions, by loosing their single outer shell electron, when they react.
They become more reactive further down the group due to shielding of the outer shell electron by the full electron shells between it and the positive nucleus.

Reaction with group 7 (17) elements (halogens)

Metal + Halogen --> Metal Halide
eg.
Lithium + Chlorine → Lithium Chloride
2Li(s) + Cl2(g) → 2LiCl(s)
