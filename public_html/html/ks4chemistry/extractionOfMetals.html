Metal Extraction

Only very unreactive metals, such as gold, are found as native metals in the ground. All the other metals, that we use, have to be extracted from compounds containing metal ions.
The metal ions have to be reduced back to metal atoms

A general example of this type of reaction is

Copper Oxide heated with Carbon produces Copper metal and Carbon Dioxide
2CuO + C → 2Cu + CO2

The half equation for this reaction is
Cu2+ + 2e- → Cu

The way extraction of metal is done depends on the reactivity of the metal

Electrolysis

Passing electric current through a molten salt will cause the metal to be deposited on the negative electrode (cathode) and other elements, from the nonmetal anion to be produced at the positive electrode(anode)

Reduction with Carbon

Heat a metal oxide with carbon(coke or charcoal) and metal and carbon dioxide will be produced
Iron is produced this way in a blast furnace
