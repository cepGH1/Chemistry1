Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the study of how energy is moved about in chemical reactions.

Chemical reactions do not make energy.

Energy is absorbed or released by chemical reactions as chemical bonds are broken or made.

Bond breaking requires energy, which has to be taken in from the surroundings. (endothermic)

Bond making releases energy, which is given out to the surroundings. (exothermic)

The amount of energy taken in or given out depends on the type of bond made or broken and upon which atoms are involved

In a chemical reaction bonds are broken then made. For the whole reaction, energy is either given out or taken in depending on whether the bonds made give out more energy than the energy that the bonds broken take in.

This can be shown on a diagram and calculated numerically.
