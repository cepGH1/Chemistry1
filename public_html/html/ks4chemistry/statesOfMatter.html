States of Matter

The most common ones are Solid, Liquid and Gas
Which state a substance is in depends on the forces of attraction between the particles in the substance and on the amount of energy these particles have(the temperature controls this).

If a solid is heated it reaches the temperature called its melting point and it melts to form a liquid.
Heating the liquid further the temperature will reach the boiling point and it will boil, to form a gas.

Sometimes, some substances can change directly from solid to gas. This is called sublimation.
Solid carbon dioxide sublimes, as does Iodine
