Equations

Word Equations

Write out the reactants and the products in words with an arrow between
Reactants → Products
Reactant + Reactant → Product + Product
Example
Sodium + Chlorine → Sodium Chloride

Formula Equations

Write the formula of each reactant and product.
Adjust the amounts of each until there are exactly the same amounts of each element on either side of the arrow. This is called balancing and equation.
Add state symbols in brackets after each to show what state of matter they are in.
Why balance equations?
No matter is created or destroyed in a chemical reaction
There must be enough atoms in the reactants to exactly make the products, although those atoms are bonded differently in the products.
Examples
2Na + Cl2 → 2NaCl

Ionic Equations

Equations that show the ions taking part in a chemical reaction

Half Equations

Equations that show electrons being gained or lost.
Na → Na+ + e-
