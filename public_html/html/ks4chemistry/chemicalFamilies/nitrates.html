Nitrates

The nitrate ion

The nitrate ion NO3- is a complex ion with a single negative charge.

It is the anion that comes from nitric acid (HNO3 ). Nearly all metal nitrates are soluble in water.

Famous Nitrates

Silver nitrate

Acidified silver nitrate solution is a standard test for halide ions.
Insoluble silver halides are produced and you can tell which halide it is from the colour of the solid precipitate

Test for chloride ions

A white precipitate of silver chloride is formed
Example
silver nitrate + sodium chloride --> silver chloride + sodium nitrate
AgNO3(aq) + NaCl(aq) --> AgCl(s) + NaNO3(aq)
This ion exchange reaction is pulled to completion as the silver chloride precipitates out of the reacting solution.

Test for bromide ions

A cream coloured precipitate of silver bromide is formed
Example
AgNO3(aq) + NaBr(aq) --> AgBr(s) + NaNO3(aq) Silver bromide is cream coloured
Silver iodide is pale yellow
