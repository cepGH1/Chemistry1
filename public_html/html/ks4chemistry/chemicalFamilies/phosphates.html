Phosphates

Phosphate

An anion made of phosphorus and oxygen atoms. Held together with covalent bonds. Phosphate ions are negatively charged.
The simplest phosphate anion is PO43-
Explanation for the charge on the phosphate ion
The charge on Phosphorus is 5+ and the charge on oxygen is 2-
1 x 5+ = 5+
So 4 x 2- = 8-
Combining these charges.....
Makes overall charge = 3-

Phosphate ion can be drawn either with the charges allocated to individual oxygen ions or (possibly more realistically) with the charge delocalised

Phosphate salts

Phosphates can look quite complicated because the positive ions don't have to all be the same.

Example : Potassium Phosphates

KH2PO4
K2HPO4
K3PO4
