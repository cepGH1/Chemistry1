Carbon 13 NMR

One in every one hundred carbon atoms is of the Carbon 13 isotope This makes no difference to the chemical reactions it does, but doesmean it has odd spin and therefore gives an NMR spectrum.
Because it is only a tiny proportion of atoms, they are very unlikely to be bonded and therefore there is no spin -spin coupling such as is seen in proton NMR

Each differently bonded carbon produces its own peak in the NMR spectrum

One peak per carbon environment, is another way of putting it

Examples
C2H6 ethane, 2 carbons but they are bonded exactly the same. One carbon environment
