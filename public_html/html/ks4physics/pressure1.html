Pressure

Force per unit area.
P = Force/Area

Example - the effect of pressure

If a person stands on your foot it can be sore. Your foot will be more sore if the person was wearing stilletto heels ( if its the heel on your foot) than if they are wearing flat shoes. This is because the stilletto heel has a very small area compared to the flat shoe heel. The same force (weight of the wearer) causes a lot more pressure when the wearer has the stilletto heels on.

Gas Pressure

If you squeeze the same amount of a gas into a smaller volume the pressure increases.
P x V = constant
If the volume is increased, but no extra gas added, the pressure is reduced
[This is only always true so long as the temperature stays the same]

Pressure in liquids

Pressure due to a column of liquid
P = height x mass x g / volume
This is an alternative way of writing force/area
