Earth's Early Atmosphere

  • Early Earth had very many volcanoes
  • Radioactive decay of radioactive elements causes the heat that keeps the rocks in the earths mantel molten. Even today this is why there is magma and lava in volcanoes
  • The atmosphere formed from gases that were erupted from volcanoes
  • The first atmosphere was mostly carbon dioxide, with water vapour and small amounts of other gases. The first atmosphere had little or no oxygen in it

How do we know this?

Modern day volcanoes produce lots of carbon dioxide, sulphur dioxide but no oxygen.
The atmosphere of Mars is mostly carbon dioxide.

Why did the atmosphere change to the mixture of gases we have today

Early life forms began to photosynthesise.
This process produced oxygen while using up carbon dioxide
Carbon dioxide also became dissolved in the newly formed oceans
