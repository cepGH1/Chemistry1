Earth's Atmosphere

78% Nitrogen N2
21% Oxygen O2
1% Argon Ar
0.04% Carbon dioxide CO2
Carbon dioxide is vital to life on earth, but also a threat to life on earth.
Carbon dioxide is used in photosynthesis to produce glucose.
In low amounts carbon dioxide traps enough heat in the earth's atmosphere to prevent huge falls in temperature during night time.
However as carbon dioxide levels rise temperatures and energy in the atmosphere rise.
If temperatures rise more than just a few degrees then the earth may become uninhabitable.
Argon is an inert gas (group 20)
Oxygen, also vital to life on earth.
Used in respiration to give animals and plants energy to move and grow.
