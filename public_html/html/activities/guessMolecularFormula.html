Working out the molecular formula from the empirical formula

If you know the Empirical Formula and the relative molecular mass of a molecule then you can work out the Molecular Formula

  1. Calculate the relative formula mass of the empirical formula
  2. Divide the relative molecular mass by the relative formula mass of empirical formula
  3. Multiply all the numbers of atoms in the empirical formula by this number

Examples

1. Empirical Formula CH3 Relative Molecular Mass 30

Relative Atomic masses C =12, H = 1
Relative Formula Mass of the Empirical Formula = (3 x 1) + (1 x 12) = 15
Relative Molecular Mass = 30
Relative Molecular Mass/Relative Formula Mass of the Empirical Formula = 30/15 = 2
This number 2 is the number that the molecular formula was divided through by to get the empirical formula
Multiply through the empirical formula by 2 to get the molecular formula
C1x2H3x2 = C2H6
The molecular formula is C2H6

2. Empirical Formula CH2O Relative Molecular Mass 60

Relative Atomic Masses C = 12, H = 1, O = 16
Relative Formula Mass of the Empirical Formula = 1xC + 2xH + 1xO = (1x12) + (2x1) + (1x16) = 12 + 2 + 16 = 30
Relative Molecular Mass = 60
Divide RMM/RFM = 60/30 = 2
Multiply the empirical formula through by 2
C1 x 2H2 x 2O1 x 2 = C2H4O2
The Molecular Formula is C2H4O2

3. Empirical Formula C2H5 Relative Molecular Mass 58

Relative Atomic Masses C = 12, H = 1
Relative Formula Mass of the empirical formula = (2x12) + (4x1) = 29
Relative Molecular Mass = 58
Divide RMM/RFM = 58/29 = 2
Multiply empirical formula by 2 = C2 x 2H5 x 2 = C4H10
Molecular Formula is C4H10
